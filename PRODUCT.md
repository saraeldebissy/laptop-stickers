# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers and designers who land on this from Sara's portfolio or through sharing. They're in a casual discovery mode — not hunting for a tool, just stumbling in. They should feel a flicker of nostalgia (sticker culture, dev humor) and genuine delight without it ever feeling forced.

## Product Purpose

A drag-and-drop sticker toy on a photorealistic MacBook lid. The interaction IS the product. It exists to showcase Sara's design and front-end craft — but the showcase only works if the experience feels genuinely fun, not like a demo dressed up as fun.

## Brand Personality

Effortless. Considered. A little irreverent.

## Anti-references

- Over-designed portfolio flex: too many animations, too many transitions, too clearly trying to impress. If it looks like a Awwwards submission, it's gone too far.
- Generic dark mode SaaS: flat dark utility UI with no character.
- Cute/bubbly: pastel, rounded, child-like energy is wrong for the dev sticker aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't announce** — the craft should be visible in the details, not telegraphed by flashy effects.
2. **Nostalgia through specificity** — get the aluminum texture, the sticker placement logic, the peel animation right. Real references beat stylized ones.
3. **Restraint earns the pops** — quiet background so the stickers can be the loudest thing.
4. **Fun is a constraint** — every interaction decision must pass the "is this actually enjoyable?" test, not the "does this look impressive?" test.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No stated requirements. Keyboard users and screen readers are not the primary concern for this toy, but avoid actively breaking them.
